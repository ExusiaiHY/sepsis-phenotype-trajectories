# Multimodal Machine Learning in Image-Based and Clinical Biomedicine: Survey and Prospects

**Source**: International Journal of Computer Vision (Springer, April 2024)
**URL**: https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s11263-024-02032-8
**Access**: Paywalled (Springer) - summary from abstract and search results
**Relevance**: Major survey on multimodal ML in biomedicine

---

## Overview

Comprehensive survey navigating the landscape of multimodal ML, focusing on medical image analysis and clinical decision support systems. Covers challenges and innovations in multimodal representation, fusion, translation, alignment, and co-learning.

## Key Topics Covered
- Multimodal representation learning
- Fusion strategies (early, intermediate, late)
- Cross-modal translation
- Alignment between modalities
- Co-learning approaches

## Key Findings
- Multimodal approaches consistently outperform unimodal methods
- Data biases remain a persistent challenge
- Scarcity of large-scale multimodal biomedical datasets limits progress
- Auxiliary mechanisms (contrastive learning, modal adapters) increasingly layered on fusion families

## Fusion Family Insights
- Intermediate fusion enables richer cross-modal interaction but is more sensitive to missing/low-quality inputs
- Modular late-fusion designs generalize more robustly across clinical settings
- Contrastive learning frameworks enforce semantic consistency across modalities

## Key Takeaways for Our Project
- This is the most comprehensive survey on multimodal ML in biomedicine
- Fusion taxonomy (representation, fusion, translation, alignment, co-learning) provides organizing framework
- Late fusion robustness vs intermediate fusion interaction richness is a core trade-off
